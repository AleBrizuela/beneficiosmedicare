Medicare Questions Answered

Medicare is a federal health insurance program for people age 65 and older, regardless of income or health status. It's also available to some younger people with disabilities and those with end-stage renal disease. Medicare has four parts: Part A (hospital insurance), Part B (medical insurance), Part D (prescription drug coverage), and optional Medigap or Medicare Advantage plans that provide additional coverage.
You become eligible for Medicare at age 65. Your initial enrollment period begins three months before your 65th birthday and ends three months after. However, you should enroll during this period to avoid potential penalties. If you delay enrollment without qualifying for an exception, you may face higher premiums for life. Additionally, you can make changes during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 - December 7) or Special Enrollment Periods if you qualify.
Medicare Advantage (Part C) is an all-in-one alternative to Original Medicare. It's offered by private insurance companies and includes Part A, Part B, and usually prescription drug coverage (Part D). These plans often have lower premiums but may have network restrictions and require prior authorization. Medigap is supplemental coverage that works with Original Medicare to help pay costs that Medicare doesn't cover, like copayments and deductibles. You can see any doctor accepting Medicare nationwide.

That depends on your situation. Original Medicare covers a significant portion of healthcare costs, but beneficiaries are responsible for deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance. If you want more predictable out-of-pocket costs, a Medigap plan may be right for you. If you prefer lower premiums and don't mind network restrictions, Medicare Advantage might be better. We help you evaluate both options based on your health, budget, and preferences to make the right choice.
Yes, you can change your Medicare plan, but there are specific times when you can do so. The main opportunity is during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 through December 7) when all beneficiaries can make changes that take effect January 1. Some people qualify for Special Enrollment Periods if they experience qualifying life events like moving, losing other coverage, or changes in health status. We help you understand when you're eligible to make changes and help with the process.
The Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) runs from October 15 through December 7 each year. During this time, anyone with Medicare can review, compare, and change their coverage for the following year. This includes switching between Medicare Advantage and Original Medicare, changing Medigap plans, or updating your Part D prescription drug plan. Changes made during AEP take effect January 1. We recommend reviewing your options annually to ensure your plan still meets your needs and budget.
